# NASA Psyche Spacecraft Parts Report

This document details the main components required to represent the NASA **Psyche Spacecraft**, including each part's **quantity**, **function**, and **placement** on the spacecraft.
This dccument will be used by me as a golden source to create the assets required for the mini game c.

---

## 1. Spacecraft Bus (Main Body)

- **Quantity:** 1
- **Function:** Serves as the main structural body housing electronics, instruments, propulsion tanks, and control systems.
- **Placement:** Central core of the spacecraft; connects all other subsystems (solar arrays, booms, thrusters).

---

## 2. Solar Array Wings

- **Quantity:** 2 wings (each made of 5 panels)
- **Function:** Generate electrical power for propulsion and onboard systems.
- **Placement:** Attached to opposite sides of the spacecraft bus; deploy outward in an “X” configuration.

---

## 3. Xenon Propellant Tanks

- **Quantity:** 7
- **Function:** Store xenon gas used as fuel for the Hall-effect ion thrusters.
- **Placement:** Mounted inside the spacecraft bus near the propulsion module.

---

## 4. Hall-Effect Thruster Nozzles

- **Quantity:** 4
- **Function:** Provide propulsion using electrically charged xenon ions (solar electric propulsion).
- **Placement:** Rear of the spacecraft bus on gimbal mounts for direction control.

---

## 5. Instrument Boom (GRNS & Magnetometer)

- **Quantity:** 1 boom (2m long) with 2 sensors
- **Function:** Holds instruments away from spacecraft to reduce interference.
- **Placement:** Extends from one side of the spacecraft bus.

---

## 6. Multispectral Imager (Cameras)

- **Quantity:** 2 (one primary, one backup)
- **Function:** Captures optical images for geological and compositional mapping.
- **Placement:** Mounted on the front-facing surface of the spacecraft bus.

---

## 7. Gamma-Ray & Neutron Spectrometer (GRNS)

- **Quantity:** 1
- **Function:** Measures gamma rays and neutrons to determine surface elemental composition.
- **Placement:** Attached to the instrument boom end, facing away from the spacecraft.

---

## 8. Magnetometer Sensors

- **Quantity:** 2 sensors
- **Function:** Measure magnetic fields to study asteroid core properties.
- **Placement:** Mounted along the same boom as the GRNS; one mid-boom, one outer end.

---

## 9. Antenna Dish (X-band Communication)

- **Quantity:** 1
- **Function:** Handles communication with Earth and assists in gravity science.
- **Placement:** Top or side of the spacecraft bus.

---

## 10. DSOC Laser Transceiver Module

- **Quantity:** 1
- **Function:** Demonstrates deep-space optical (laser) communication technology.
- **Placement:** Protrudes from the spacecraft side with an 8.6-inch aperture telescope.

---

## 11. Thermal Louver Panels

- **Quantity:** 4–6 (estimated)
- **Function:** Regulate spacecraft temperature by radiating or retaining heat.
- **Placement:** Mounted on spacecraft bus radiator surfaces.

---

## 12. MLI (Multi-Layer Insulation) Blankets

- **Quantity:** Multiple panels
- **Function:** Protect spacecraft from extreme temperature changes.
- **Placement:** Covers most exterior surfaces of the bus and some components.

---

## 13. Attachment Brackets & Harnesses

- **Quantity:** Many (for connections)
- **Function:** Provide mounting interfaces and secure wiring or mechanical connections between parts.
- **Placement:** Across the bus structure; connecting booms, arrays, sensors, and tanks.
